Context

For the last years, my best high school friend I would always take a picture of us in our ugly sweaters. Except this year, there was no holiday party for us to go to. This lead to.

It was a dark and stormy night. For some reason, it was actually raining in bone-dry California. Occasional sirens were droning, fading into the distance, probably because people forgot how to drive when a single drop of water hit the roads. I would know because I was one of those people.

Fresh from the heartbreak of losing my Elephant car and tired of watching the same youtube videos over and over again, I looked my housemate in the eye. I wanted to do something fun!

My housemate and I were bored. I had a realization that I haven’t coded “for fun” in 6 months ever since I started work. Each night I would write unit tests for the product I was working on. The purpose of this project was two-fold: to see if I still had the passion/drive to do something for myself, and rediscover the spark that drove me to program earlier.

I remember my commencement speaker for College (Leslie Odom Jr) uttered a quote that I truly resonated with. Something along the line of “Every day we try to recreate the spark we first felt”

For me, that first spark was my first hackathon at CodeDay Silicon Valley 2015. I created an iphone app about a little dinosaur within 24 hours. I had a mentor that taught me the basics of command line and version control. I got a little wooden box inscribed with the words “Best Game.” I was hooked.

I kept on coding. I wanted to make things for fun. Not for money. But on pure unbridled passion. Of making and constructing things.
